HR Analytics – Hiring Cycle & Attrition Analysis

Strategic Workforce Planning | Hiring Efficiency | Business Impact

Executive Summary

This HR analytics project evaluates the end-to-end hiring lifecycle to identify bottlenecks affecting workforce availability, productivity, and talent competitiveness. The analysis highlights prolonged hiring timelines, high dependency on backfills, and attrition-driven hiring inefficiencies, followed by actionable, data-driven recommendations.

Hiring Cycle Performance

  • Average Time to Hire (New Candidates): 134 days
  • Average Time to Hire (Experienced Candidates): 113 days
  • Average Time to Issue Offer (New): 91 days
  • Average Time to Fill (Experienced): 72 days
  • Post-Offer Joining Time (New Hires): 43 days
  • Post-Offer Joining Time (Backfills): 41 days

Key Analytical Insights

  • The hiring lifecycle is significantly prolonged, delaying workforce availability and impacting operational productivity.
  • Hiring young talent is particularly challenging, reflected by the high average hiring time of 134 days.
  • Slow offer rollout and acceptance timelines increase the likelihood of losing high-quality candidates to competitors.
  • Extended joining periods raise the risk of candidate dropouts and unrealized productivity.
  • Backfills account for 17% of total hiring, indicating persistent attrition-related hiring pressure.
  • One job category alone recorded 77 backfills, signaling structural retention challenges.

Business Impact Assessment

  • Delayed workforce onboarding affects service delivery timelines
  • Increased recruitment costs due to repeated backfill hiring
  • Loss of top talent due to slow hiring decisions
  • Productivity gaps caused by prolonged vacant positions

Strategic Recommendations

  • Adopt AI-driven hiring automation to accelerate resume screening, shortlisting, and interview scheduling.
  • Build and maintain a pre-screened talent pool to reduce time-to-fill for recurring roles.
  • Introduce pre-approved salary bands to minimize offer negotiation delays and speed up acceptance.
  • Enhance candidate engagement through structured communication during long hiring cycles.
  • Standardize a 90-day notice period policy to reduce joining variability.
  • Define a fixed offer acceptance window to avoid prolonged decision timelines.
  • Strengthen employee retention through career development and upskilling initiatives.
  • Track attrition trends proactively to anticipate backfill needs.
  • Offer early joining incentives such as sign-on bonuses or flexible joining options.
  • Optimize vendor and recruitment partner strategy to improve hiring speed and candidate quality.
